Most people reach for paracetamol or ibuprofen the moment a headache strikes. If you're experiencing frequent headaches, there's likely an underlying cause — and chiropractic care may be the answer.

## Types of Headaches We Treat

Not all headaches are the same, and understanding the type you're dealing with is the first step toward effective treatment.

### Tension Headaches

The most common type, tension headaches feel like a tight band of pressure around the forehead or the back of the head. They're often caused by:

- Prolonged poor posture
- Stress and muscle tension in the neck and shoulders
- Jaw clenching or teeth grinding
- Eye strain from screen use

### Cervicogenic Headaches

These headaches originate from problems in the cervical spine (neck) and refer pain upward into the head. They're frequently one-sided and accompanied by neck stiffness. Causes include:

- Misalignment of the upper cervical vertebrae
- Joint dysfunction in the neck
- Muscle tightness in the suboccipital region
- Previous neck injury or whiplash

### Migraines

While migraines are more complex, research shows that spinal misalignment — particularly in the upper cervical region — can be a contributing trigger. Many migraine sufferers experience significant improvement with regular chiropractic care.
