# Posture and Scoliosis: How Chiropractic Care Can Improve Your Spinal Health

Good posture is one of those things everyone knows they should have — but few people actively maintain. Over time, poor posture becomes structural. What starts as a habit becomes a pattern that your spine adapts to, leading to pain, fatigue, and reduced quality of life.

For those with scoliosis — an abnormal lateral curvature of the spine — postural challenges are even more pronounced. At Thrive Chiropractic, we help patients with both postural dysfunction and scoliosis improve their alignment, reduce discomfort, and build lasting spinal health.

## The Impact of Poor Posture

Poor posture doesn't just look bad — it creates a cascade of problems throughout the body:

- **Chronic neck and back pain** from muscles working overtime to support misaligned joints
- **Headaches** triggered by tension in the upper neck and shoulders
- **Reduced lung capacity** — a rounded upper back compresses the chest cavity
- **Digestive issues** — slouching can compress abdominal organs
- **Fatigue** — your body expends more energy maintaining a misaligned posture
- **Joint degeneration** — uneven loading accelerates wear and tear

### Common Postural Problems

- **Forward head posture** — head sits in front of the shoulders (often from screen use)
- **Rounded shoulders** — shoulders roll forward, tightening the chest muscles
- **Thoracic kyphosis** — excessive rounding of the upper back
- **Anterior pelvic tilt** — the pelvis tilts forward, increasing lower back curve
- **Lateral imbalance** — one shoulder or hip sits higher than the other

## Understanding Scoliosis

Scoliosis is a sideways curvature of the spine, most commonly diagnosed during adolescence. It ranges from mild (barely noticeable) to severe (causing significant deformity and functional limitation).

### Types of Scoliosis

- **Idiopathic scoliosis** — the most common type, with no known cause (typically develops in adolescence)
- **Degenerative scoliosis** — develops in adults due to age-related spinal changes
- **Congenital scoliosis** — present from birth due to vertebral abnormalities

### Signs of Scoliosis

- Uneven shoulders or shoulder blades
- One hip higher than the other
- A visible curve in the spine when bending forward
- Rib prominence on one side
- Clothing that hangs unevenly

- **Slowing progression** — maintaining spinal function can help prevent the curve from worsening
- **Addressing compensatory issues** — scoliosis often causes secondary problems in other parts of the spine and body
