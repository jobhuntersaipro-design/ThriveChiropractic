## What Is Sciatica?

Sciatica refers to pain that travels along the path of the sciatic nerve — the longest nerve in your body. It runs from the lower spine, through the buttock, and down the back of each leg. When this nerve is compressed or irritated, it can produce:

- **Shooting or burning pain** from the lower back into the leg
- **Numbness or tingling** in the leg or foot
- **Weakness** in the affected leg
- **Pain that worsens** with sitting, coughing, or sneezing

## Common Causes of Sciatica and Hip Pain

Sciatica isn't a condition in itself — it's a symptom of an underlying problem. The most common causes include:

- **Herniated or bulging discs** — disc material presses on the sciatic nerve root
- **Spinal misalignment** — vertebral subluxations in the lumbar spine alter nerve pathways
- **Piriformis syndrome** — the piriformis muscle in the buttock tightens and compresses the sciatic nerve
- **Pelvic misalignment** — an uneven pelvis can shift weight distribution and irritate surrounding structures
- **Degenerative disc disease** — age-related wear narrows the spaces where nerves exit the spine
