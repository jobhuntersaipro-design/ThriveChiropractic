# Neck Pain: Causes, Consequences, and How Chiropractic Can Help

If you've ever woken up with a stiff neck or felt that familiar ache creep in after a long day at your desk, you're not alone. Neck pain is one of the most common musculoskeletal complaints we treat at Thrive Chiropractic — and it's becoming more prevalent every year.

## The Rise of "Tech Neck"

We spend more time looking at screens than any generation before us. Phones, laptops, tablets — they all encourage us to tilt our heads forward and down. This forward head posture places enormous strain on the cervical spine. For every inch your head moves forward from its neutral position, the effective weight on your neck increases by roughly 4.5 kilograms.

Over weeks and months, this leads to:

- **Cervical misalignment** — vertebrae shift out of their proper position
- **Muscle fatigue and tension** — the muscles at the back of your neck work overtime to support your head
- **Nerve irritation** — misaligned vertebrae can compress or irritate the nerves exiting the cervical spine
- **Reduced mobility** — stiffness sets in, making it painful to turn or tilt your head

## Common Causes of Neck Pain

While screen time is a major contributor, neck pain can also result from:

- **Sports injuries** — whiplash, tackling, or impact-related trauma
- **Poor sleeping position** — an unsupportive pillow or awkward sleeping angle
- **Workplace ergonomics** — monitor height, chair position, and desk setup
- **Stress** — emotional tension often manifests as physical tension in the neck and shoulders
- **Underlying spinal conditions** — disc issues, arthritis, or structural abnormalities

## How We Treat Neck Pain at Thrive Chiropractic

Our approach starts with understanding exactly what's going on in your cervical spine. Through careful assessment — including posture analysis and spinal examination — we identify the specific misalignments contributing to your pain.

### Gonstead Adjustments for the Cervical Spine

The neck is a delicate area that requires precision. The Gonstead method excels here because it focuses on specificity — adjusting only the vertebrae that need correction, with controlled, gentle force. This targeted approach:

- Restores proper alignment to the cervical vertebrae
- Relieves pressure on irritated nerves
- Reduces muscle tension and spasm
- Improves range of motion

### Beyond the Adjustment

We also work with you on practical strategies to prevent neck pain from returning. This includes guidance on workstation ergonomics, sleeping positions, and simple stretches you can do throughout the day.

## Don't Ignore Neck Pain

It's tempting to push through neck pain and hope it goes away on its own. Sometimes it does — but more often, untreated neck issues compound over time, leading to chronic pain, headaches, and even referred pain down the arms.
